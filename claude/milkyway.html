The Log Truth

What this map is hiding from you

Stars are not dots. They're points.
On this map, 1 unit ≈ 1,000 light-years. The Sun's real diameter is 0.000000009 light-years. Every star you see is drawn roughly 10 billion times too large. At true scale, you are looking at pure darkness — no star would illuminate a single pixel.
Star sizes here = importance, not physics
Sirius appears big because it's famous and close. Betelgeuse appears big because it's a supergiant. But at this scale, a supergiant and a red dwarf are equally invisible. We size them by significance so you can find them.
The galaxy shape is real — the particles aren't
Spiral arms, the central bar, the core bulge — all based on real observations. But the individual particles represent millions of stars each. The Milky Way has 100–400 billion stars. We're showing about 80,000 particles.
Distances between stars: incomprehensibly vast
Even in our "dense" galactic neighborhood, the nearest star is 4.24 light-years away. If the Sun were a marble in New York, the nearest other marble would be in Los Angeles. The space between stars is almost perfectly empty.
